Caribbean Airlines has announced the end of its Caribbean Cafe buy-on-board service just over seven years after it was introduced as the airline moves to reduce operating costs. In an internal memo to staff, the airline said the change took effect yesterday. The Caribbean Cafe service allows passengers to select routes to purchase branded merchandise and locally made products from Trinidad and Tobago and Jamaica while in flight. The airline says the service has now been discontinued until further notice. Guardian Media also reports that Caribbean Airlines will close its merchandise outlet, the Caribbean Shop, yesterday.
