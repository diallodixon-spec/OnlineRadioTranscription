**Interview with Mrs. Janelle Millet-Jones on CSEC Information Technology Syllabus**

In a recent interview on the A-Team Radio Show, Mrs. Janelle Millet-Jones, an expert in information technology, shared her insights on the CSEC Information Technology syllabus. The conversation, which was conducted by host Damon Scarlett, delved into the various aspects of the syllabus, including problem-solving, programming languages, and web page design.

Mrs. Millet-Jones emphasized the importance of information technology in today's world, stating that "IT has a lot of opportunities for us. It's changing, it's taking over, and it's for us to get involved." She encouraged students to study hard and practice problem-solving, as it is a crucial aspect of the subject.

The interview also touched on the topic of web page design, which is a new addition to the syllabus. Mrs. Millet-Jones explained that students are required to create a web page, and they are given the freedom to choose their own authoring tools, such as Wix or Google Sites. She noted that this aspect of the syllabus allows students to be creative and showcase their skills.

When asked about the grading process, Mrs. Millet-Jones explained that students are graded on the content, design, and functionality of their web pages. She also mentioned that the syllabus does not require students to publish their websites, but rather to create a link to an external source, such as an Instagram page.

The conversation also covered the topic of programming languages, with Mrs. Millet-Jones noting that the syllabus allows schools to choose their own programming languages. She mentioned that some schools are using Python, while others are using Pascal or Java.

In her final words, Mrs. Millet-Jones encouraged students to take the subject seriously and to practice regularly. She also emphasized the importance of seeking help when needed and reinforced the idea that information technology is a subject that can lead to many opportunities.

The interview provided valuable insights into the CSEC Information Technology syllabus and highlighted the importance of this subject in today's technology-driven world. As Mrs. Millet-Jones noted, "Information technology as a subject at CSEC prepares us for that. So I would say study hard. It's an easy subject. It's easy enough subject to get a grade one in."
