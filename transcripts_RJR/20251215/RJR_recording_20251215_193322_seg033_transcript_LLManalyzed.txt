20251215 193322 The Office of Utilities Regulation, OUR, is advising that customers of the Jamaica Public Service, JPS, currently receiving service will see a 7% increase on their December bills. The utilities regulator says this adjustment was anticipated as the JPS had to rely on more expensive fuel sources for generation due to the unavailability of natural gas in the period leading up to and immediately after Hurricane Melissa. The bills cover November's consumption. The OUR further explained that, among other things, the hurricane resulted in an approximately 30% reduction in sales, which contributed significantly to the increase in the fuel and independent power producer IPP rates. The OUR says together these factors may translate to a big hike in the November bills. It noted, however, that to avoid shocking customers with one high bill, the JPS in consultation with the OUR opted to defer some of the increase in fuel and IPP costs and recover it by spreading it over the upcoming months.
