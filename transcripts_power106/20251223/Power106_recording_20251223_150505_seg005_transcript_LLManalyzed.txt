20251223 150505 My father, a backyard farmer, planted sorrel to be reaped at Christmas. Now, with new varieties, sorrel is available all year. My mother's fruits for the fruitcake were soaking in wine and rum all year. A store-bought fruitcake was considered a travesty. Green goonga peas was also commonly available at Christmas, along with citrus fruits. And then there was the house cleaning. Everything had to be spic and span and quite new. Then Christmas shopping downtown with a partner draw to buy the new curtains, sheets, clothes, hat, shoes, etc. I still recall a Christmas shopping trip downtown with my mother in the 1960s. On Christmas Day, children may get one present, a toy or a book and Christmas ham. No dinner would be complete without the cured baked ham. All the new clothes came out on Christmas day for church.
