An emotional appeal for honesty and accountability were made yesterday during the funeral of University of the West Indies student Victoria Brown Handley in Mandeville. During the service at Andrews Memorial Church, a preacher urged authorities to ensure that the investigation into the 20-year-old's death is handled openly without concealment. Speaking through tears, she said families deserve answers and that justice must be allowed to take its course. Her remarks drew strong responses from members of the congregation. Brown-Hanley, a final year student, was found dead in her room at George Lane Hall at the UMona campus on October 16th of this year. An autopsy later revealed that she died as a result of strangulation.
