Minister of Tourism Edmund Bartlett has been appointed chair of a new high-level Caribbean tourism organization CTO committee geared at creating regional strategies to manage the supply aspect of tourism. Among other things, the committee will focus on deepening linkages between tourism and sectors such as agriculture, manufacturing, and the creative industries to ensure that Caribbean communities retain a larger share of tourism earnings. The high-level committee will include representatives from Anguilla and Tigum Barbuda, the Bahamas, Barbados, Belize, British Virgin Islands, Dominica, Grenada, St. Lucia, St. Kitts and Nevis, St. Vincent and the Grenadines, and Turks and Caicos Islands. Via a press release, Minister Bartlett stated that the Committee's efforts will be focused on developing businesses, creating high-quality jobs for local communities, and enhancing the resilience of Caribbean economies.
