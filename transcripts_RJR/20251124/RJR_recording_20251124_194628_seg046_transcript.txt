What we're seeing in areas like Content Manchester, parts of St. Elizabeth, Hanover, Clarendon and St. James and others is not the result of broken pipes, damaged rivers or a mysterious leak on the ground. It's groundwater, naturally stored water beneath the earth's surface, rising to the point where it breaks through the soil. Think of groundwater as water that has soaked deep into the earth over time. It fills the spaces between soil and rock, eventually collecting in large underground pockets known as aquifers. These aquifers act like massive natural reservoirs.
