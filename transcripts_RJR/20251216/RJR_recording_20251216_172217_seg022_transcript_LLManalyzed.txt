The Bank of Jamaica says it will draw a further $36 billion from the financial system through a 6 percent per annum fixed rate certificate of deposit auction tomorrow. Of the amount on offer, $34.2 billion will be allocated to private, institutional, and individual investors on a competitive basis, while $1.8 billion will be reserved for public sector institutions on a non-competitive basis. The Certificate of Deposit will mature on January 16 next year, at which time interest will be paid at the annual rate of 6%. The JSC index advanced by 807 points today, while the junior market gained 42 points. A total 107 stocks traded, 41 advanced, 40 declined, and 26 traded firm. Trans-Domacan Highway was the volume leader, with 3.5 million units, or 29 percent of total volume.
