Rear passenger side visibility is poor due to a thick D-pillar added for structural rigidity. Mazda includes blind spot monitoring, but it can't fully compensate for the design compromise. It's frustrating because nearly everything else is executed exceptionally well. Ride quality is where the CX-90 surprised critics. The suspension is tuned with European-style control, firm, composed and stable without feeling harsh. It absorbs rough roads cleanly and reduces body motion, leading to less passenger discomfort than softer competitors. Then there's capability. The CX-90 can tow up to 5,600 pounds, outperforming many three-row rivals. Owners report stable performance even when towing at maximum limits.
