The Bank of Jamaica is reporting that Jamaica's international reserves remain robust despite the worsened external position caused by Hurricane Melissa. They currently stand at a historical high of $6.3 billion U.S. dollars up to mid-December 2025. The island traffic authority is reporting that 366 persons have died in traffic crashes since January. This is a 3% increase over the number of persons killed in crashes for the similar period last year. The rural agricultural development authority is advising farmers to prepare themselves for the beet-army worm season, which usually occurs between February and June each year. And 10 outstanding primary exit profile students from St. Catherine have been awarded bursaries valued at $20,000 each by the Friends of Region 6. That was the GOJ Minute.
