Significant pothole formation, scoured pavements, and roadways stripped by floodwaters are some of the issues to be addressed under the graded overlay Emergency Road Rehabilitation Go program. The $1.6 billion road program will roll out on a phased basis starting in December, with additional packages coming on stream early in the new year. The GO program will address main and heavily traveled roadways that are vital for daily commute, commerce, access to schools, health facilities, farms, and town centers. Minister with Responsibility for Works Robert Morgan says the program is designed to provide targeted fast-track interventions on services damaged while longer-term reconstruction solutions are being finalized.
