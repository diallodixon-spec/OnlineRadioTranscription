Mockien passed away on December 31, after a brief period of illness. She was 97 years old. In a statement Wednesday afternoon, the Culture Minister described Mockien as the doyen of contemporary media, whose contributions were instrumental in shaping the landscape of media education and practice in Jamaica and the wider Caribbean. Minister Grange further stated that the late media icon was a woman of class who distinguished herself in all she did for the many years she spent in public life. A pioneer in radio broadcasting, Alma Mark Yen was in the very first class of the Institute of Mass Communication established at the University of the West Indies in 1975. She went on to become a broadcast journalism trainer and lecturer at the Caribbean Institute of Media and Communication, CARMAC, and was in charge of the Mona Campus
