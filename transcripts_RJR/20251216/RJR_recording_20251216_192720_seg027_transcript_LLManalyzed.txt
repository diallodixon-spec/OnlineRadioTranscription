20251216 192720 The latest management foozles at Parliament, including rookie bungling of procurement contracts, suggest the need for a deeper review and probably overhaul of Parliament's operational system. The editorial says in the face of this month's revelations in an investigative report by the Auditor General and the September 2023 claim by the Integrity Commission that the former House Speaker Marisa Dalrymple-Philibert was irregularly paid a motor vehicle upkeep while she was assigned a vehicle from Parliament. Perhaps the Chief Personnel Officer of the Public Services Commission should insist on special management and systems training for the management and supervisory staff in Parliament. The training should include roles and responsibilities of accountable officers, the fiduciary duty and responsibility of care that public servants have to taxpayers when dealing with state assets.
