The announcement comes five weeks into cleanup operations with the NSWMA, reporting that about 15,000 truckloads of debris have been cleared so far. Officials say hundreds of thousands more loads remain, following the passage of the Category 5 storm. The additional trucks were commissioned at the NSWMA headquarters in St. Andrew and are expected to help fast-track work in communities that were hardest hit. Authorities say the new units will be dedicated specifically to debris removal, allowing other trucks that were reassigned after the hurricane to return to regular garbage collection. Government officials have also indicated that discussions aren't going to secure more heavy equipment to support the cleanup process as the volume of debris remains significant.
