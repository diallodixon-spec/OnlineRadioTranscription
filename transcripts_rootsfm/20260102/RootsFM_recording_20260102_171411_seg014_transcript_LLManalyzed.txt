However, weeks later, the tides seemed to have turned for the better, and amidst the inflow of relief support, approximately 200 farmers and fisherfolk in Treasure Beach, St. Elizabeth, have made quite the haul. Over $7 million in supplies and equipment. Part of Hurricane Melissa recovery activities, the supplies were provided by the National Commercial Bank Foundation and in partnership with the Ministry of Agriculture, Fisheries and Mining. Agriculture Minister Floyd Green says the initiative provided about 100 farmers with vouchers valued at $50,000 each for fertilizer, weed whackers, sprays and other tools. Another 100 fishermen received fish pots, wires, ropes and floats.
