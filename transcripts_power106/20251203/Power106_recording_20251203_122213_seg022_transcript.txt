The Jamaica Constabler Force, JCF, is advising the public to be wary of a fraudulent scheme targeting individuals who have sold or are planning to sell their motor vehicles. JCF says criminals are contacting vehicle sellers via telephone, pretending to be police officers, and claiming that the vehicles they sold are stolen property. The JCF adds that the criminals, under the pretense of helping the sellers to avoid prosecution or reimbursing the supposed buyer, are demanding immediate cash payments from the sellers. Additionally, the scammers also enlist unsuspecting rideshare drivers to collect cash from victims and transport the funds to various drop-off locations. Superintendent of Police assigned to the Counterterrorism and Organized Crime Investigation Branch, CTOC, Victor Barrett, says no legitimate police operation involves soliciting or collecting money from citizens to settle criminal matters or reimburse third parties.
