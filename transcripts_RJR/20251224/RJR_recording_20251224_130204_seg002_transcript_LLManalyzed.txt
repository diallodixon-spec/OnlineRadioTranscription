Health and Wellness Minister Dr. Christopher Tufton says a policy is being worked on to upgrade the system for the disposal of medical waste to prevent further pile-up at healthcare facilities. Our new Centre recently reported that there was a backlog of uncollected waste from Victoria Jubilee Hospital in Kingston and St. Jacob Health Centre, which stores waste for the Spanish Town Hospital in St. Catherine. Medical waste includes items such as tests or treat patients. Used-to-test-or-treat patients include bandages, gloves, swabs and sharp implements like needles and scalpels. The waste is not dumped at any disposal site, but is taken to a specialized facility. Dr. Tufton says while the waste has been cleared from hospitals and health centers, the buildup will recur without a consistent removal process.
