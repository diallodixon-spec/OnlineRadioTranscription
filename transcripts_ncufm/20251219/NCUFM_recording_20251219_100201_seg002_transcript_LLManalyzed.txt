20251219 100201 In health news, the Westmoreland Public Health Department have been implementing corrective measures to safeguard food safety and sanitation standards across the parish following Hurricane Melissa. Speaking at a recent monthly meeting of the Westmoreland Municipal Corporation, Chief Public Health Inspector for Westmoreland, Steve Morris, indicated that some 658 food handling establishments were checked. He says over 39,000 kilograms and over 800 liters of food were condemned at food handling establishments due to damage from the hurricane. He informed that tourism properties were also included in the inspection program as the sector moved through the recovery phase. He added that some operations have since resumed
