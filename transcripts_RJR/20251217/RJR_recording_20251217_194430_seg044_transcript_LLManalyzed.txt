In a letter sent on November 18, the NWC confirmed that it stopped billing in the hardest hit areas started in December. That means no service charge will appear on December bills, which only reflect November's usage. The NWC says the suspension will remain in place for as long as service disruptions continue. JPS also confirmed in correspondence dated December 3 that customers without electricity for an entire billing period will not have to pay the customer charge. They will still receive a bill, but it will show zero consumption along with any outstanding arrears. The OUR says it welcomes both decisions and will continue working with the utilities to ensure fair treatment for affected customers.
