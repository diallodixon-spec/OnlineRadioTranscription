The Ministry of Agriculture, Fisheries and Mining has received relief inputs from agricultural retailer Hypro to further support farmers as they rebuild from Hurricane Melissa. The items, which are valued at approximately $5.5 million, include chicks, feed, animal feed, supplements and chicken wire. The company also provided support for vegetable and livestock farmers, including seeds, agricultural chemicals, fertilizers and feed for goats, pigs and lairs. Portfolio Minister Floyd Green says the gesture will ease the financial burden on poultry farmers while supporting the agricultural sector's restoration. Feed is one of the single largest input costs for poultry farmers and disruptions to feed availability or affordability can delay recovery long after the storm has passed.
