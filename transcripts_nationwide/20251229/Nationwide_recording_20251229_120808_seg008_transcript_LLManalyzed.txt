20251229 120808 35,000 Jamaica Public Service Company JPS customers in the parishes worst affected by Hurricane Melissa recently had their electricity restored. Energy Minister Dara Vaz provided the update on his social media on Sunday. He disclosed that electricity was restored to 35,672 JPS customers in West Milan, St. Elizabeth, Hanover, St St. James and Trelawny in December. He added that the Black River Police Station has been reconnected to the grid in the last 24 hours. According to the Energy Minister, over 102,000 JPS customers in the worst affected parishes were still without electricity up to a month ago. He pointed out that full restoration for these customers was set for June 2026. But Minister Vaz noted that due to the government's intervention, providing JPS with a 150 million US dollar loan, work has been accelerated and is continuing throughout the holidays.
