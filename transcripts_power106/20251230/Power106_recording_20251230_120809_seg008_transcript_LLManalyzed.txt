The administration of St. Jacob High School in Spanish Town, St. Catherine is seeking support from the Minister of Education and private sector after a fire destroyed the drama and the dance rooms and box and day. The fire which started at the school about 5.30 p.m. was reportedly lit on an adjacent property. Radio-Jamaica News has informed that the fire was being used to clear debris. Principal of St. Jacob High, Colette Price, says it is suspected that embers from the fire were blown onto the board structure that housed the performing arts and technical drawing rooms. She says the technical drawing area was spared. Mrs. Price says the loss to the performing arts rooms is significant as the subject areas are mandatory for the entire school population of 1,560 students.
