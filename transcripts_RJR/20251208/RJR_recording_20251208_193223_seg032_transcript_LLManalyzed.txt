The National Housing Trust, NHT, has expanded the range of documents needed to verify identity and property ownership for persons seeking assistance under its Hurricane Melissa relief initiatives. Assistant General Manager for Corporate Communications and Public Affairs Duane Burbick says the NHT will now accept property tax records, land diagrams and notarized declarations from a Justice of the Peace confirming the history and undisturbed occupancy of the land. Additionally, the NHT's internal contributor database will be utilized to support identification verification where possible, which will reduce delays for individuals who may have lost essential documents during the hurricane. Meanwhile, the NHG continues to encourage persons to take advantage of its comprehensive set of disaster response measures to assist contributors and mortgages whose homes or livelihoods were impacted by the recent hurricane.
