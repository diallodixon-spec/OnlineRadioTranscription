Malabar said early assessment would allow accurate information to follow students throughout the education system, helping schools provide the right support at an early age. He noted that conditions such as autism are often identified too late after teachers have already struggled to manage classroom challenges. One of the major barriers, he said, is parental resistance to assessment, as some parents are reluctant to accept that their child may need additional support. Malabra believes a mandatory framework will remove this obstacle and ensure timely intervention once concerns are flagged by schools. The Ministry of Education has acknowledged the issue of undiagnosed learning challenges, noting that special needs services are currently available at dozens of locations island-wide.
