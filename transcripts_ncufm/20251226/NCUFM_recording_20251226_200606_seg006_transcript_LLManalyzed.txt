20251226 200606 He knew that unless the people took a decided stand to serve the Lord with all their hearts, they would continue to separate themselves farther and farther from him. While a portion of the Hebrew hosts were spiritual worshipers, many were formalists. No zeal or earnestness characterized their service. Some were idolaters at heart, who would be ashamed to acknowledge themselves as such. This solemn covenant was recorded in the Book of the Law. To be sacredly preserved, Joshua then set up a great stone under an oak that was by the sanctuary of the Lord. And Joshua said unto the people, Behold, this stone shall be a witness unto us, for it hath heard all the words of the Lord which he spake unto us. It shall be therefore a witness unto you, lest you deny your God.
