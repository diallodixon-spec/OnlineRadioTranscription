The PIAJ says the bill would allow limited emergency withdrawals and contributed holidays and contribution holidays, measures it argues are urgently needed to help affected families and businesses. Speaking in a press release today, PIAJ President Sanya Goff says discussions on emergency access to pension savings have gone on for more than 20 years, but the current situation demands immediate attention. She notes that thousands of Jamaicans are under sudden financial pressure, yet there is still no legal framework to allow controlled access to a portion of their pension funds. The PIHA is also calling on the government to prioritize passage of provisions or move them separately if necessary and has pledged its support to ensure the changes are implemented quickly and responsibly.
