Health and Wellness Minister Christopher Tufton says he is hopeful that hospitals in Western Jamaica damaged by Hurricane Melissa will be largely restored by the end of January, reducing the need for field hospitals currently supporting patient care. Speaking with the media, Tufton said repair work is now underway at several facilities, including the Black River Hospital in St. Elizabeth, Noel Holmes Hospital in Hanover, Falmouth Public General Hospital in Trelawny, and Savannah Lamar Public Hospital in Westmoreland. Each facility sustained varying levels of damage during the passage of the category.
