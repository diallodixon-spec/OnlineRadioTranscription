Good morning. In the Rural Headline News at this hour, the shortage of certified sanitized water trucks is affecting water delivery to several communities in northeast St. Elizabeth. Member of Parliament Zolika Jess told Radio Jamaica News over the weekend that securing certified trucks to serve the constituency has become increasingly difficult. She noted that the issue is further compounded by the fact that the parish has only two National Water Commission loading bays. The NWC requires that all trucks transporting domestic water be certified and sanitized. However, with limited availability, some residents have been forced to rely on independent truckers who collect water from springs for household use.
