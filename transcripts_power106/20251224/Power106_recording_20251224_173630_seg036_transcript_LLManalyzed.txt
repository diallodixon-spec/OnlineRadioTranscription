Dear Nola, I cannot tell you how surprised we were to hear from you so soon again and to receive your lovely present of two turtle doves. You really are too kind. At first the Patridge was very jealous and suspicious of the doves and they had a terrible row on the night the doves arrived. We had to send for the vet. But the birds are ok again and the stitches is due to come out in a week or two. The vet's bill was eight pound, but the mother is over her annoyance now and the doves in the pathridge are watching the telly from the pear tree as I write. Yours ever, Gobnet. Day three.
