The minister says a full-week surge is planned, with 600 assessors to be deployed across the six parishes most severely impacted by the storm. Officials say the additional manpower is needed to complete outstanding assessments within the operational period. To date, just over 56,000 households' assessments have been completed, while an estimated 150,000 are required overall. The Labor Ministry says support has been bolstered through assistance from World Food Program funded assessors, members of the Jamaican Defense Force, National Service Corps, and the redeployment of the Labor Ministry staff from other parishes.
