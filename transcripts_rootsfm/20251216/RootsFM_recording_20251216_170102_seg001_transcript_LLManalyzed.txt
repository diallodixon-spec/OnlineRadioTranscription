The Prime Minister also urged residents currently housed at the Petersfield High School shelter to work with social workers and other support agencies. He noted that the shelter is expected to close by the end of December to allow for the reopening of school in the new year. Dr. Holness said the government is exploring longer-term solutions for residents who have lost their homes, including land and housing options, for those now without anywhere to live. He added that particular attention is being given to vulnerable groups such as the elderly and shut-ins. Teams from the Ministry of Local Government and the Ministry of Labour and Social Security, he said, have been meeting with affected residents to tailor assistance based on individual circumstances.
