President of the Inter-Secondary Schools Sports Association, Keith Wellington, is calling for urgent government support to assist Western schools as the high school track and field season moves into full swing. With the Montego Bay Sports Complex still unable to host track and field events, Western schools will once again be forced to travel to St. Catherine for major meets ahead of the Issa Grace Kennedy Boys and Girls Athletics Championships in March of next year. Key preparatory events, including the Western Championships and the Milo Western Relays, are again scheduled for the G.C GC Foster College due to the poor condition of the Catherine Hall track. Speaking on a podcast, Wellington said the prolonged situation has placed an unfair burden on Western schools, many of which are already dealing with increased costs
