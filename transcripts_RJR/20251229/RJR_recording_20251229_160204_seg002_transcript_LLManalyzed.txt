20251229 160204 The Jamaica Constabulary forces reminding members of the public that gun salutes are illegal and deserting firearm holders to refrain from the practice during celebrations. The JCF says besides being unlawful, the practice of gun salutes poses serious risks, including injury to people and damage to property, and may even result in death. The police say people who engage in or facilitate this practice will be prosecuted to the fullest extent of the law, wherever these incidents are observed and reported. And the Island Traffic Authority says it is concerned about the number of road fatalities for 2025. As at December 29, a total of 373 lives have been lost in 324 fatal collisions across the island. Road fatalities have increased 3% when compared with the similar period for 2024, representing a difference of 10 fatalities.
