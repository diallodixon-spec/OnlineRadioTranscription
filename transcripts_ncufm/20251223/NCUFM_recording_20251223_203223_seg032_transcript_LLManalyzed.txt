According to the prophet of his trade, he will not enjoy, for he has oppressed. He has abandoned the poor. He has seized a house, but did not build it. Because he has not known satisfaction in his stomach, he lets nothing escape that he desires. There is nothing left after he has eaten. Therefore his prosperity will not endure. In the fullness of his excess, he will be in distress. All of misery's power will come upon him. When his stomach fills up, God will send his burning anger upon him, and he will let it rain down upon him as his food. He will flee from an iron weapon, but an arrow of bronze will pierce him. He draws it forth, and it comes out.
