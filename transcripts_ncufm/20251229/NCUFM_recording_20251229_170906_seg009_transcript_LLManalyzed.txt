20251229 170906 In regional news, the government of St. Vincent and the Grenadines has announced plans to provide housing for Union Island residents still displaced after Hurricane Beryl. Prime Minister Godwin Friday says returning families to their communities is key to recovery and economic restart in the southern Grenadines. Speaking to The Nations, Friday said many residents remain on mainland St. Vincent because of limited housing, despite basic services being restored on Union Island. Hurricane Beryl, which struck in July last year, damaged more than 90 percent of buildings on Union Island, forcing thousands to relocate. The prime minister criticized the slow pace of recovery under the previous administration and said his government is now working to make housing available, including temporary solutions if needed.
