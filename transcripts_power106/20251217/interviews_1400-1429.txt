**Nurse of the Year Shares Inspirational Story**

In a heartwarming interview, Nurse Feudie Collins, the 2025 Lascaux Nurse of the Year, shared her remarkable journey and dedication to the nursing profession. With over 24 years of experience, Collins has made a significant impact in the lives of countless individuals, and her story is a testament to the power of compassion and hard work.

When asked how she felt about being recognized as Nurse of the Year, Collins expressed her gratitude and humility, stating, "I'm feeling so good to know that I can represent not only my country, but my profession, my institution, Percy Jonah Hospital, and the Nurses Association of Jamaica." She also shared her passion for nursing, saying, "The greatest joy is to be able to go to help, to offer what others do not have, to put a smile on somebody's face, to offer hope and to allow them to know that yes, somebody cares and we are here to help."

Collins' journey into nursing was not straightforward, as she initially wanted to pursue a career in languages. However, due to financial constraints, she discovered nursing and has never looked back. She emphasized the importance of family support, saying, "My family is very important, and being married, I'll be celebrating 31 years next week. They are my strong support, and at times when I feel down, they pray for me, they encourage me, and they would go with me on some of the journey."

As a pediatric nurse, midwife, and nursing supervisor, Collins has demonstrated her commitment to the profession and her country. When asked what advice she would give to young people considering a career in nursing, she said, "Nursing has so much to offer. There are advancements, different fields in nursing that you can utilize, you can upgrade yourself and serve so well in your country. You don't have to be afraid; we have persons to help you, to guide you, and to guide you into the specialty that you would want to do in order to better yourself."

Collins' story is a shining example of dedication, perseverance, and passion for the nursing profession. Her inspirational journey serves as a reminder of the importance of recognizing and rewarding excellence in healthcare, and her legacy will continue to inspire future generations of nurses.

**Lasco Chairman's Award Honors Outstanding Police Officers**

In a separate event, the Lasco Chairman's Award ceremony honored outstanding police officers who have demonstrated exceptional courage, initiative, and compassion in their service to the community. Professor Rosalie Hamilton, CEO of the Lasco Chin Foundation, emphasized the importance of recognizing and rewarding excellence in policing, saying, "We are driven by the belief that real change begins with people, with us, not some set of people out there, all of us."

The ceremony recognized the top six finalists for the 2025 Lasco JCF Saluting Our Heroes Police of the Year Award, including Constable Madik Bartley, Corporal Rejane Holt, Detective Corporal Chadrick Williams, Constable Shantel Powell, and Detective Corporal Livy Evans. The winner of the prestigious Lasco Chairman's Award will receive $175,000 in cash prizes, an Alaska gift package, and other tokens of appreciation.

The event highlighted the importance of community policing and the individual actions of officers who choose service over force. Professor Hamilton emphasized the shared vision and responsibility between the Lasco Chin Foundation and the Jamaica Constabulary Force to improve the lives of the Jamaican people, saying, "This shared vision is also a shared responsibility to help shape a Jamaica where security, opportunity, and dignity are accessible to all."
