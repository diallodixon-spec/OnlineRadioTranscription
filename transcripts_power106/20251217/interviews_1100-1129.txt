State Minister of National Security, Juliet Cuthbert Flynn, Urges Citizens to Prioritize Security During Christmas Season

In a recent interview on Radio Jamaica's "Talk with Substance" program, State Minister of National Security, Juliet Cuthbert Flynn, emphasized the importance of prioritizing security during the Christmas season. Minister Flynn noted that while the police are working diligently to combat crime, citizens must also take precautionary measures to ensure their safety.

The Minister advised citizens to stay off their cell phones, especially when walking or crossing the street, and to be vigilant when using ATMs or entering public spaces. She also encouraged people to walk in groups, particularly at night, and to keep their keys ready to quickly enter their vehicles if necessary.

Minister Flynn expressed concern about the safety of children during the holiday season, urging parents and guardians to be extra vigilant and to keep a close eye on their kids, especially in high-traffic areas. She also encouraged neighbors to check on each other, particularly the elderly, to ensure their safety and well-being.

When asked about a recent report of an alleged rape, Minister Flynn assured that the police are investigating the incident and urged anyone with information to come forward. She emphasized the importance of citizens speaking up and working together with the police to solve crimes.

The Minister concluded by wishing everyone a safe and merry Christmas, and encouraged citizens to take responsibility for their own security and the security of those around them.
