The initiative will provide direct financial support to 50,000 people, marking a shift from emergency food relief to longer-term recovery. The program follows the distribution of food assistance to more than 123,000 vulnerable residents in St. Elizabeth, St. James, Trelawny, and Westmoreland, and is now being expanded to Hanover. The cash assistance is expected to help families rebuild livelihoods and strengthen food security, while also stimulating local economic activity as businesses reopen across Western Jamaica. The World Food Program is also supporting longer-term recovery efforts, including restoring farmlands andies and facilitating 1.9 million US dollars in insurance payouts to vulnerable households through Jamaica's national social protection system.
