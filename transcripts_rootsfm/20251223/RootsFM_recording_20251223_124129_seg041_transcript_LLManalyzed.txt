Health Promotion and Education Officer Gerald Miller says the signs are being placed at popular water sites where people frequently gather, often without realizing the health dangers involved. He explained that many of the locations targeted are areas where residents bathe, wash or gather for recreation, despite the possible presence of disease-causing organisms in the water. Mr. Miller noted that ongoing water shortages have also pushed some communities to rely on rivers for domestic use, increasing the risk of illnesses. Health officials have warned that bathing or playing in contaminated water can allow germs to enter the body through the skin.
