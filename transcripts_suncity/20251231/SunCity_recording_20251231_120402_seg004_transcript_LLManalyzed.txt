The Bank of Jamaica says it is intensifying efforts to expand access to its central bank digital currency, Jamdex, as economic activity in western Jamaica continues to be disrupted by Hurricane Melissa. Speaking at the VOJ's quarterly monetary press conference on December 22, Governor Richard Biles said the central bank is pushing banks to upgrade point-of-sale machines to accept Jamdex, allowing for wider public use. Hurricane-related power and telecom outages have forced increased reliance on cash, prompting the BOG to inject emergency funds into the system. Governor Biles warned that Jamaica's dependency on cash is unsustainable.
