The government of Jamaica has announced a hard stop date of January 15, 2026, for the relief on off-import duty and general consumption tax on selected items brought into the island following Hurricane Melissa. In a statement issued by the Ministry of Finance and the Public Service, the government said the extension was granted in response to challenges faced during the Christmas period, including delays affecting shippers, freight forwarders, brokers, and port operators. These constraints, officials said, prevented some relief supplies from arriving before the original December 31st deadline. Hurricane Melissa, a Category 5 storm, caused widespread damage across Jamaica, prompting an outpouring of support from Jamaicans at home and abroad.
