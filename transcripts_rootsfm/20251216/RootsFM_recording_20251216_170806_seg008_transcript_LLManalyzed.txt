State Minister Crystal Lee says expectant and new mothers are among the most vulnerable groups during and after a disaster and the initiative is designed to ensure they continue to receive care, guidance and support. Under the program, health workers are going to directly go into communities to check on mothers and babies and to encourage regular health visits. The outreach has already been rolled out in Black River in St. Elizabeth, as well as Savannah Lamar and Grange Hill in Westmoreland. Plans are in place to expand the program to St. James, Hanover, and St. Anne. With island-wide implementation to follow, Minister Lee says the initiative focuses on routine antenatal and postnatal care, education on childbirth and newborn care, and access to mental health resources.
