Energy Minister Daryl Vaz is fuming at the existing Jamaica Public Service JPS license as customers brace for a 7% increase in their December electricity bills. Minister Vaz was reacting to the increase in electricity bills approved by the JPS by the OUR. He was speaking at a media briefing at the ministry's office in St. Andrew on Tuesday. JPS customers are set to face a 7% increase in their electricity bills this month. The OUR explained that the increase is as a result of JPS having to use more expensive fuels to generate electricity in the run-up to and aftermath of Hurricane Melissa.
