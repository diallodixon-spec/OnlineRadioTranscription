We now turn our attention to what's been happening within the Caribbean region. Three Caribbean nations are among a new group of countries facing expanded travel restrictions to the United States, following an announcement by the Trump administration on Tuesday. Antitiga and Barbuda and Dominica have been placed under partial travel restrictions, while Haiti remains subject to a full ban on entry to the U.S. The measures are set to take effect on January 1st and form part of a broader expansion of U.S. immigration controls. U.S. officials say the decision is linked to concerns about national security, screening and vetting systems, visa overstays, and cooperation with deportation requests.
