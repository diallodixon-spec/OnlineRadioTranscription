Hoteliers are optimistic about the 2025-26 winter tourist season, which began on Monday after the tourist industry suffered a setback following disruptions caused by Hurricane Melissa. The hurricane left many hotels and attractions along the west and north coasts in varying stages of destruction. But President of the Jamaica Hotel and Tourist Association, Christopher Jarrett, says tourism is rebounding faster than anticipated. Speaking yesterday on the morning agenda on Power 106, Mr. Jarrett said the recovery is signaling renewed confidence in Jamaica as a leading travel destination.
