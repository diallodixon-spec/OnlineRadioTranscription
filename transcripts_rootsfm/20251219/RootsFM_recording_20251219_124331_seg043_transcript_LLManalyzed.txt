20251219 124331 Health authorities are reviewing the future of three major coastal hospitals badly damaged by Hurricane Melissa as the government considers whether rebuilding with stronger protections or relocating inland offers the safest long-term option. On the review are the Black River Hospital in St. Elizabeth, Noel Holmes Hospital in Hanover and Falmouth Hospital in Trelawny. The Ministry of Health and Wellness says the process has moved beyond emergency repairs and is now focused on a build-back-better approach guided by climate risk, engineering standards and cost. Health Minister Dr. Christopher Tufton says Hurricane Melissa exposed the serious vulnerabilities of health facilities located along the coastline, particularly as storms become more intense and frequent.
