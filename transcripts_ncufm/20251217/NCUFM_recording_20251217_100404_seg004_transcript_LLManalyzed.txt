Speaking at the handover ceremony, Portfolio Minister Dr. Christopher Tufton said the equipment will support 18 EIR pilot projects currently underway across the island. According to the minister, having one integrated system for immunization data across the country will allow health authorities to clearly identify gaps, monitor communities or institutions with low response rates and planned targeted interventions. He says the ability to analyze data in real time also means the health system can respond more quickly and efficiently to public health needs.
