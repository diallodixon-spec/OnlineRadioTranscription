Speaking at the commissioning of 26 Justices of the Peace in Point Hanover, Chuck said the program has proven highly effective since its introduction and will now be reviewed for further expansion. He told the gathering that a Joint Select Committee of Parliament will examine amendments to the Child Diversion Act next year to allow more referrals and broader access. Under the proposed changes, guidance, counsellors and other community stakeholders could be empowered to refer children who are showing early signs of behavioral problems, including those at risk of gang involvement. Chuck said this would allow intervention before young people become deeply entrenched in criminal activity.
