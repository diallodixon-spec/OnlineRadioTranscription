The US yesterday launched a strike against militants linked to the Islamic State group in northwestern Nigeria, where militants have sought to establish a foothold. The US military said camps run by the group in Sokoto State, which lies on Nigeria's border with Niger, were hit, adding that an initial assessment suggested multiple fatalities. U.S. President Donald Trump said the Christmas Day strike was powerful and deadly and labeled the group terrorist scum, saying it had been targeting and viciously killing primarily innocent Christians. Nigerian Foreign Minister Yusuf Metama Toga told the BBC that it was a joint operation and had nothing to do with a particular religion.
