Many had the chance to put on safety gear for the first time and learn the basics of skateboarding, while others explored street art painting as part of the wider cultural activities. President of the Jamaica Skate Culture Foundation and tour organizer Remy Walter says he was pleased with both the turnout and the enthusiasm shown by the children. Walter believes that with serious development, Jamaica has the potential to one day produce an Olympic-level skateboarder. The Jamaican skate tour is expected to travel across the island in 2026. Walter also highlighted international support for the initiative. He noted that France financed the mobile skate park, Germany supporting the tour, and the University of Technology Jamaica has provided space for assembling the park. Looking ahead Walter says the foundation hopes to build permanent and small community skate parks across Kingston and other major towns in a bid to grow the sport.
