20251230 172019 It's not trend-driven loyalty, it's trust. Now, let's address the elephant in the room, the design. The Outback isn't conventionally attractive. Its shape is polarizing, almost like a minivan that decided to hit the gym. Subaru knows this, yet refuses to redesign it. Why? Because their philosophy prioritizes function over style. And that decision has paid off. The Outback uses Subaru's symmetrical all-wheel drive system, which is fundamentally different from most AWD setups. Power is split 50-50 front to rear all the time, not just when traction is lost. This provides consistent grip in all conditions, but it's more expensive to build. Most competitors avoid it to protect profit margins. Subaru doesn't. The result is impressive. A Consumer Reports reliability score of 89, with owner-reported problems 68% lower than average.
