20251216 120909 A Jamaican citizen was yesterday sentenced to five years in a U.S. prison for participating in a long-running scheme to defraud an elderly California woman. Thirty-six-year-old Dwayne Anderson of a Hanover address here in Jamaica pleaded guilty to one count of wire fraud in September this year. The sentence of 63 months' imprisonment will be followed by three years of supervised release. Anderson was also ordered to pay more than $181,000 in restitution. He was arrested on July 11 in 2024 by Jamaican authorities based on a U.S. indictment and was extradited. According to court documents, Anderson undertook a scheme to defraud an American woman from 2010 to 2017. Anderson, using fake names, contacted the victim by telephone, text message, and email, and falsely informed her that she had won millions of dollars in sweepstakes. In total, the victim paid Anderson more than $181,000.
