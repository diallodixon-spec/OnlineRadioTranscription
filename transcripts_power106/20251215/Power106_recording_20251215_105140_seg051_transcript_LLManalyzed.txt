The prices of food and non-alcoholic beverages account for 35.7% of the wage in the basic basket that is used to measure the inflation rate and the agricultural sector has been completely devastated by the hurricane and hence the high food prices. This therefore means that inflation cannot be reduced without the boosting of agricultural output. But the players in the sector, which have never had access to affordable financing, need it now more than ever. And as a result, there must be a lowering of interest rates to the sector, among other forms of assistance to boost output in order to reduce inflation, given the sector's heavy weight on the inflation index.
