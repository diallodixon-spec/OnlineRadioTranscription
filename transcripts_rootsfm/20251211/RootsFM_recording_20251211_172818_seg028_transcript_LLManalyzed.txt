Investigators from the lottery scam task force conducted coordinated raids after tracking complaints linked to a network of individuals who allegedly convinced victims they had won prizes that required upfront payments. During the operations, police seized electronic devices, documents, and large sums of cash believed to be connected to the scheme. The suspects are being questioned and are expected to face charges related to obtaining money by false pretence and conspiracy to defraud. Investigators say the victims were mostly senior citizens who were contacted by phone and pressured into sending money through remittance agencies. The police have renewed calls for families to educate older relatives about scam tactics that continue to evolve.
