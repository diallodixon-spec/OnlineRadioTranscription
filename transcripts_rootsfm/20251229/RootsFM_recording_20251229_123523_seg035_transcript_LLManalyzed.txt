The Jamaica Constabulary Force is urging members of the public to refrain from engaging in gun salutes as the country prepares to bring in the new year. In a statement issued today, police reminded Jamaicans that the firing of guns into the air is illegal and extremely dangerous. The JCF warned that the practice poses a serious risk to life and property as falling bullets can cause severe injury and or death to unsuspecting persons. The police say anyone found engaging in or facilitating gun salutes will face prosecution as officers will be maintaining heightened visibility across communities and businesses during the holiday period.
