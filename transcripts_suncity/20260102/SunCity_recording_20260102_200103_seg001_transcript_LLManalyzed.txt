Now, several eyewitness accounts during Hurricane Melissa, specifically in the Black River area of St. Elizabeth, describe a topsy-turvy scenario, where the sea seemingly sought to trade places with the land, regrettably, as strong storm surges swept inland, their retreat took many a fisherman's livelihood along with them. However, weeks later, the tides seemed to have turned for the better, and amidst the inflow of relief support, approximately 200 farmers and fisherfolk in Treasure Beach, St. Elizabeth, have made quite the haul. Over $7 million in supplies and equipment. Part of Hurricane Melissa recovery activities, the supplies were provided by the National Commercial Bank Foundation and in partnership with the Ministry of Agriculture, Fisheries and Mining.
