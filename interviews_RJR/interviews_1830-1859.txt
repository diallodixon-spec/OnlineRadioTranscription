**Interview with George Davis and Janet Silvera: Reflecting on 2025's Biggest Stories**

In a recent interview on Radio Jamaica, Deputy Executive Producer and News Editor at Nationwide News Network, George Davis, and Senior Journalist at The Gleaner, Janet Silvera, joined the host to discuss the biggest stories of 2025. The conversation began with condolences to George Davis, who worked closely with the late Donald Oliver.

Janet Silvera reflected on the year, citing the election, crime reduction, and Hurricane Melissa as the top three stories. She expressed her personal experience with the hurricane, having visited affected areas and having nightmares about the devastation. Silvera also mentioned the emotional toll of 2025, with many losses, including the death of people she knew.

George Davis shared his perspective on the year, highlighting the general election, which was marked by intense campaigning and negativity, but ultimately did not result in election-related violence. He also noted the significant reduction in crime, with the homicide number below 700, a remarkable achievement. Davis mentioned the arrival of Messi in Jamaica and the Reggae Boys' performance in the World Cup qualifiers as notable sports stories.

The conversation also touched on the impact of Hurricane Melissa, with George Davis describing it as "unfathomable" and Janet Silvera sharing her personal experience of having two sisters who almost drowned in the hurricane. Silvera emphasized the importance of having a strong support system and the need for people to come together to rebuild and recover.

The interview provided a thought-provoking reflection on the major stories of 2025, highlighting the resilience and strength of the Jamaican people in the face of adversity. As George Davis aptly put it, "The biggest room in the world is the room for improvement," emphasizing the need for continuous growth and development.
