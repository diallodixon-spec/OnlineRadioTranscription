Jamaica public service company customers who have power will be paying more as the light and power company today confirmed a seven percent hike on December bills tied to November consumption. The Office of Utilities Regulations says the adjustment was anticipated as JPS had to rely on more expensive fuel sources for generation because natural gas was unavailable in the period leading up to and immediately after Hurricane Melissa. Nikinsky Robinson tells us more. In the immediate aftermath of the hurricane, President and Chief Executive Officer of JPS Hugh Grant had warned of a possible upward movement in bills.
