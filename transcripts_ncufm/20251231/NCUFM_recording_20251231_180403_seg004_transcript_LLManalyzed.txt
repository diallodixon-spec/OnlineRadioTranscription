And finally, in boxing Japanese sensation, Naoya Inoue retained his undisputed Super Bantamweight title with a dominant point victory over Alan David Picasso Romero in Riyadh, Saudi Arabia. Widely regarded as one of the pound-for-pound best in the sport, Inoue controlled the contest from start to finish, showcasing sharp combinations, speed, and ring intelligence across all 12 rounds. The judges scored the bout 119-109, 120-108, and 117-111 as the 25-year-old Picasso struggled to cope with Inoue's relentless pressure and bodywork. Despite extending his perfect professional record to 32 wins with 27 by knockout, Inoue admitted afterwards he was disappointed with his own performance. The victory keeps the door open for a potential blockbuster.
