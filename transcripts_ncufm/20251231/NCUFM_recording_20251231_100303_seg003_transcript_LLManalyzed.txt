In an interview with JS News, health promotion and education officer for Westmoreland, Gerald Miller, says influenza vaccination should ideally be taken before the start of the flu season, but he says a person should not delay as protection remains important throughout this period. He explained that vaccination can be administered during routine health care visits or hospitalization once the vaccine becomes available, helping to prevent missed opportunities. Mr. Miller says several priority groups are strongly encouraged to receive the vaccine during the season, including health care workers.
