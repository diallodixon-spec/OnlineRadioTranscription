The initiative targets 50,000 Jamaicans who are not enrolled in PATH Poor Relief, the social pension program, the national insurance scheme, or similar safety net initiatives. It will also benefit those who are not able to access the government's reverse income tax credit. A second tranche of payments is scheduled for January 2026. Labour Minister Pernod Charles Junior says the programme is designed to ensure inclusion by providing immediate relief. He says it will also link beneficiaries to key services such as healthcare, skills training, employment support and social security assistance. Community outreach activities have already been held in several parishes, including St Mary, Portland, Manchester Manchester, Kingston and St. Andrews, St. Elizabeth and Westmoreland, with more engagements planned for the new year.
