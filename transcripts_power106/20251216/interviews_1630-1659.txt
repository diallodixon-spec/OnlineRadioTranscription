**Interview with Zimbabwe**

In a recent interview on Power 106 FM, Zimbabwe expressed his concerns about the plight of animals in Horton Grove, Hanover, after Hurricane Melissa. He stated that the animals are still traumatized from being left out in the open during the hurricane. Zimbabwe emphasized the need for better treatment and care of animals, saying "it's not right" and "we are feeling better than that." He also called on the Prime Minister and opposition to take action, saying "do what you can do" and "confirm, do what you can do best."

Zimbabwe's emotional appeal highlighted the need for empathy and compassion towards animals affected by natural disasters. His words, "I feel so sorry for the animals," underscored the importance of considering the well-being of all creatures in the aftermath of a hurricane.

**Interview with Sir P**

Sir P, a caller on the show, discussed the issue of electricity rates with the host, Mr. Blake. He expressed concern about the increase in rates and the use of IPP (Independent Power Producers) by JPS (Jamaica Public Service). Sir P questioned the need for rate increases, given that JPS has a contract with LNG (Liquefied Natural Gas) suppliers. He also mentioned that the cost of using IPP is almost three times the normal rate, which could lead to higher electricity bills for consumers.

Sir P's comments highlighted the need for transparency and accountability in the management of electricity rates. His concerns about the impact of rate increases on consumers underscored the importance of considering the welfare of citizens in decision-making processes.
