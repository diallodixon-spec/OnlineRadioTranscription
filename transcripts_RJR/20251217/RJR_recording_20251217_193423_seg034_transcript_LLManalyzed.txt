And five persons with disabilities have received grants of $300,000 to help them obtain prosthetic devices to improve their quality of life. The assistive aid grants were provided by the Jamaica Council for Persons with Disabilities, JCPD, which is also providing scholarships to two disabled persons, allowing them to pursue tertiary education. The JCPD's Margaret Moody scholarships are valued at $350,000 each. The funds were handed over at a ceremony at the JCPD head office in Kingston yesterday. During the event, Labour and Social Security Minister Colonel Charles Junior commended the continued efforts of the JCPD to achieve greater inclusion within society for persons with disabilities.
