Health and Wellness Minister Dr. Christopher Dofton notes that the remaining 10 percent of public pharmacists which suffered major structural damage are currently delivering services through a mobile unit. Since November 1, mobile units have filled approximately 4,440 prescriptions for over 3,500 patients in 62 remote communities across Western Jamaica. Since the December 11th and Friday December 12th, the mobile units will be operating in St. James, Cambridge, Hanover, Chester Castle, and Mount Pito, Westmoreland, Cornwall Mountain, and Big Woods, St. Elizabeth, New Market Health Center, Vineyard Square. And up to December 7, some 292,000 prescriptions have been processed, bringing a number of prescriptions filled on par with a similar period in October, demonstrating the effectiveness of the NHF response.
