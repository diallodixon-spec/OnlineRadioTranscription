**National Surfing Champion Eddie Sharma Bedford Inspires with His Journey**

In an interview on Power 106 FM, national surfing champion Eddie Sharma Bedford shared his inspiring story of perseverance and determination. Bedford expressed his confidence in Jamaica's surfing abilities, stating that the country stands out in the water and has the potential to compete on an international level. He emphasized the importance of believing in oneself and having support, saying, "Sometimes I think the thing that we need to work on as Jamaicans is realizing and believing in ourselves that we are on the level, like undoubtedly we're on the level."

Bedford's passion for surfing is contagious, and he encouraged listeners to get involved in the sport, especially the youth. He said, "Just get the kids out there surfing and come enjoy the beach. That's what it is." The host praised Bedford as "very inspirational," and it's clear that his dedication to his craft is making a positive impact on his community.

**Jamaica Drops Association Prepares for National Championship**

In another interview on Power 106 FM, Vice President McCoy and Treasurer Oren Thompson of the Jamaica Drops Association discussed the upcoming national championship. Despite the challenges posed by Hurricane Melissa, the association is pushing forward with the tournament, which will feature four groups of players, including a top masters group, an intermediary group, a beginners group, and an open group.

Thompson expressed his gratitude for the support of the draft community, saying, "We are grateful for that, and that is what also gave us the strength and the initiative to push on through with the national tournament." The tournament will be held on December 21st at the Port Rhodes Club in Discovery Bay Center, and the association is encouraging participation from players of all ages and genders.

The association has also introduced a relaxed entry fee of $1,000, down from $2,500, to encourage more players to participate. Thompson explained, "Because what you need now is participation rather than collection." The tournament will feature medals, trophies, and certificates for the winners, and the association is seeking sponsorship to support the event.
