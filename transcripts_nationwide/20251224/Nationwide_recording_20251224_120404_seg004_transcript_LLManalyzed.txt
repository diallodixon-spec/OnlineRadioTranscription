For the period December 12 to 22, the central bank made just under $18 billion more available to the public. This resulted in a net increase of $5.3 billion in the overall money supply. The percentage increase was lower than the 3.4% recorded over the same period in December 2024. Over the wider period from December 1 to December 22 this year, $12.5 billion more were put into circulation after currency returns to the central bank were factored out. It's an increase in the cash available to the public of 2.9%. This was below the BOJ's earlier projection of 4.2% and lower than the 4.5% seen during the corresponding period in 2024.
