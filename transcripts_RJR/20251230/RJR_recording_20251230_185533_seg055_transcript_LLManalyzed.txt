The PAJ also highlighted that his work inspired a new generation of broadcasters and extended condolences to his family, colleagues, friends and audiences. Meanwhile, the Jamaica Football Federation, JFF, has also expressed its deep condolences. In a statement issued on Tuesday, the JFF described Oliver as a stapler voice and familiar face in sports commentary, particularly in football, whose work helped to shake the local and regional games coverage. The federation said his commentary style brought excitement and color to broadcasts with vivid descriptions of goals, players and matchday atmosphere that captivated audiences. Interim General Secretary of the JFF Roy Simpson paid tribute to Oliver describing him as the Jamaican Peter Jury and said listening to his commentary was a joy.
