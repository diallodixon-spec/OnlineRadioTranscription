The NWC says when these materials build up in sewer lines, they can solidify and block the flow of wastewater, leading to backups, overflows, property damage and environmental harm. Wastewater manager at the NWC, Patrick Daly, says frequency of sewer blockages points to poor waste disposal practices by some businesses. He notes that this negligence is causing unnecessary disruptions and environmental concerns. In response, the NWC says it has intensified island-wide inspections of restaurants and fast food outlets, with a focus on the construction, use and maintenance of grease traps, as well as the type of waste being discharged.
