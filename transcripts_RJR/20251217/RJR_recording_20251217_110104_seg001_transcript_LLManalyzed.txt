In the Rural Headline News at this hour, warning signs are being installed near rivers and streams across Westmoreland as part of a public education campaign by the Westmoreland Health Department to reduce the risk of leptospirosis and diarrheal diseases following Hurricane Melissa. Health Promotion and Education Officer Gerald Miller said the signs target popular sites where residents bathe, recreate or collect water, often unaware of the health risks.
