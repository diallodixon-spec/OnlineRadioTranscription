The Jamaica Fire Brigade is urging Jamaicans to review and practice home fire safety plans during the Christmas season, noting that most fire related deaths occur in residences, not workplaces. Public Education Officer Superintendent Emilio E. Banks said families should conduct home fire drills, clearly explain evacuation plans to visitors, and identify assembly points and emergency contacts. He also encouraged households to have direct contact numbers for their nearest fire station to ensure faster response times. Superintendent Ebbang stressed the need for realistic evacuation plans that considered elderly or disabled family members an appeal to citizens to remain vigilant to help prevent avoidable fire tragedies during the holidays.
