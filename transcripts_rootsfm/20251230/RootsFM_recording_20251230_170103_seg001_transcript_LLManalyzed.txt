Addressing public concern about fatal police shootings, the Commissioner noted that the incidents occurred during confrontations and represented a small fraction of overall police interactions. He stressed that the Jamaica Constabulary Force remains committed to preserving life and bringing offenders before the courts whenever possible. Blake urged members of the public, particularly those involved in criminal activity, to comply with lawful instructions when confronted by police, warning that escalation often leads to tragic outcomes. He said officers continue to demonstrate restraint and professionalism. The commissioner credited collaboration between law enforcement and government partners for the improved results, saying while challenges remain, the data shows progress in reducing violent crime.
