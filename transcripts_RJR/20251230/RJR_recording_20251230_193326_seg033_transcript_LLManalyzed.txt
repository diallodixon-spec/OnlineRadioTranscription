The Ministry of Health and Wellness is assuring the public that chemicals being used in fogging activities are considered safe for members of the public. This in light of plans for fogging activities to be conducted in several communities to control the increase in the mosquito population since Hurricane Melissa. Health and Wellness Minister Dr. Christopher Tufton says fogging is a safe method for getting rid of mosquitoes that transmit dengue and other viruses, adding that the chemicals are also certified and approved by the World Health Organization, WHO. He pointed out that the insecticides have become significantly more effective and environmentally friendly over the years. The Health Minister is encouraging residents to cooperate with fogging efforts and advises individuals with allergies to minimize exposure to the mist or place a damp cloth over the nose until the mist dissipates. The WHO advises that the amount of insecticide used in fogging is very small and dispersed at quantities that are only effective at killing small insects like mosquitoes.
