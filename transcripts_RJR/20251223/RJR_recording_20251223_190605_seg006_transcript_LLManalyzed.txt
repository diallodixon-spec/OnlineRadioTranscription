And as he grew up, he felt it was obvious why society in and around Poland was becoming fragmented. Some spoke Polish, others Russian. There were Yiddish and German speakers too. Wouldn't it be better, the boy thought, if there was a shared language? If one didn't already exist, then maybe he could create one. That is not to say that everyone would only speak this language together, but it would be a helping language, an auxiliary language, as he put it, an international language, a second language for the world. This was the birth of Esperanto. Created by Ludwig Leitzar Zamenhof in 1887, there are 16 rules. Nouns end in O, adjectives A and adverbs E.
