Prime Minister Dr. Andrew Holness says the growth of the Jamaican economy by 5.1 percent in the July to September quarter of this year is a strong sign of resilience. He says the economic growth is a justification of the government's hope that 2026 will be prosperous for Jamaicans. The Statistical Institute on Wednesday said the expansion, compared to the same period last year, was driven by strong growth in the goods-producing industries, which grew by nearly 11 percent, alongside a 3.3 percent increase in the services sector. Statting said the improved performance reflected continued recovery from the impact of Hurricane Beryl in July last year, with increased activity in sectors most affected, including agriculture, mining, utilities and tourism related services.
