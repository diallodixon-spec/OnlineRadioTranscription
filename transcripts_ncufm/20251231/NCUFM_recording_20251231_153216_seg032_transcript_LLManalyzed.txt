Respiratory infections are common in babies during the cold season, ranging from mild congestion to serious illnesses like bronchitis or pneumonia. Understanding these challenges can help parents protect their little ones and seek timely medical care when needed. So here are some common respiratory challenges in the cold season. One of the most common respiratory issues in infants during cold months is bronchitis, which is usually caused by viruses such as RSV, respiratory syncytial virus. We heard a lot about RSV recently. Babies with bronchitis may have a persistent cough, wheezing, rapid breathing or mild fever. It is particularly common in infants under one year of age. Pneumonia is another serious concern.
