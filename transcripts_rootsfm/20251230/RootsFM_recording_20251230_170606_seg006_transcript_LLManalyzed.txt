20251230 170606 The Small Business Association of Jamaica is calling on the Office of Utilities Regulation and the Jamaica Public Service to reconsider a proposed 7% increase in electricity rates, warning that the move could further strain already burdened consumers and businesses. SBAJ President Garnett Reid says electricity costs in Jamaica remain among the highest globally and argues that this is not the time to raise rates, especially as the country continues to recover from the impact of Hurricane Melissa. He questioned why additional costs are being passed on to customers, noting that the power company has recorded significant profit over the years. Reid also raised concern about the impact of high energy costs on business survival, pointing that companies have relocated to other Caribbean territories with lower electricity rates.
