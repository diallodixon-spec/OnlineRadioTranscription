Antigua and Barbuda, as well as Dominica, have been placed under partial travel restrictions, while Haiti remains subject to a full ban on entry to the U.S. The measures are set to take effect on January 1st and form a broader expansion of U.S. immigration controls. U.S. officials say the decision is linked to concerns about national security, screening and vetting systems, visa overstays, and cooperation with deportation requests. In the case of Antigua and Barbuda and Dominica, the administration cited long-standing citizenship by investment programs without residency requirements as a risk factor.
