Jamaicans are being encouraged to contact the National Environment and Planning Agency, NEPA, in the event they encounter animals displaced from their normal habitats since the passage of Hurricane Melissa. Deputy Chief Executive Officer for Environmental Management and Sustainability, Anthony McKenzie, says since the hurricane, the agency has seen increased reports of displaced animals such as crocodiles. Mr. McKenzie emphasized that persons should not attempt to capture any wildlife, but rather make a report to NEPA as to relocate. Mr. McKenzie was speaking recently on the Jamaica Information Service television program, Get the Facts.
