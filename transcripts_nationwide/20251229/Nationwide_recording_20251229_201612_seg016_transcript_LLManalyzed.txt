20251229 201612 Fire trucks typically have about 4,000 liters of water, which can be pumped out in about seven minutes. Without a constant water supply, firefighters would need to make multiple trips to replenish their water. To avoid this, the Jamaica Fire Brigade, JFB, maintains fire hydrants across the island through its Hydrant Maintenance Crew. The members of this crew are responsible for mapping hydrants, assessment and repair, as well as repair and maintenance activities, including replacing parts, clearing, chipping and painting hydrants. We recently signed a memorandum of understanding with the National Water Commission, NWC, to help with the servicing and repair of some of these hydrants because the numbers that we are seeing in terms of those that are in need of servicing and repair outstrip the resources that we have internally. Commissioner of the Jamaica Fire Brigade Stuart Beckford.
