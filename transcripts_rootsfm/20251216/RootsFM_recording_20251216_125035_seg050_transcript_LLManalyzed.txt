The announcement follows the recent installation of a U.S. radar system at an airport in Tobago, which Trinidad and Tobago says is being used to assist with local crime-fighting efforts. The twin island republic lies just a few miles off the coast of Venezuela. Shortly after the announcement, Venezuelan Vice President Delci Rodriguez said her country was cancelling all energy-related agreements with Trinidad and Tobago, accusing the government of being complicit in the recent seizure of a Venezuelan oil tanker by U.S. authority. Prime Minister Kamala Prasad Besesa dismissed the accusations, describing them as false and politically motivated. She said Trinidad and Tobago has adequate natural gas reserves and continues to maintain peaceful relations with the Venezuelan people.
