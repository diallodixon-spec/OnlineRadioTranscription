Howard Schultz grew up in a working class family in Brooklyn, New York. He was the first in his family to go to university. He studied communications at Northern Michigan University, dreaming of a career in advertising. After graduation, he landed jobs in sales and marketing. Nothing glamorous, just steady work. Then one day, he walked into a small coffee company in Seattle called Starbucks. At the time, Starbucks wasn't a cafe. It only sold beans and coffee equipment. But Schultz saw something more. On a trip to Italy, he was captivated by espresso bars, places where people gathered, talked, and built community over coffee. He thought, why not bring this culture to America?
