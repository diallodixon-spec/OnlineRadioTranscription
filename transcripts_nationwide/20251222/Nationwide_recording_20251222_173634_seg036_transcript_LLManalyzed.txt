20251222 173634 the International Credit Ratings Agency S&P Global, formerly Standard & Poor's, has revised its outlook in Jamaica to stable in the aftermath of Hurricane Melissa. The outlook was published by S&P Global on Friday. Dana Davey has more. The rating is a step down from the positive outlook S&P Global gave to Jamaica after upgrading the country's global credit rating to double B in September. S&P Global says despite Hurricane Melissa delivering a significant hit to Jamaica's growth in the near term and a need to fund rebuilding, the country's solid institutions and preparedness for external shocks, in addition to an expected economic rebound, support the current rating.
