The general election, held on September 3rd, 2025, was fiercely contested but the JLP ultimately emerged victorious, securing 35 parliamentary seats compared to the People's National Party's 28. The results confirmed a shift in the balance of power, with the PNP gaining 15 seats compared to the last election, while the JLP lost 15. Despite these gains by the opposition, the outcome ensures the JLP will form the next government, marking its third consecutive term in office and the party's 10th general election victory since 1944. Opposition leader Mark Golding was the first to address the nation following the declaration of results. In a concession speech, he congratulated the JLP on its victory while underscoring the importance of protecting Jamaica's democratic traditions, quote, Jamaica's democracy is important and we must cherish it, unquote.
