Meanwhile, chairman of the UDC Norman Brown says the corporation has assessed the situation on the ground and is mobilizing resources to support businesses and residents so that the town can recover and function. The Office of Utilities Regulation, OUR, is advising that customers of the Jamaica Public Service, JPS, currently receiving service, will see a 7% increase on their December bills. The utilities regulator says this adjustment was anticipated as the JPS had to rely on more expensive fuel sources for generation due to the unavailability of natural gas in the period leading up to and immediately after Hurricane Melissa.
