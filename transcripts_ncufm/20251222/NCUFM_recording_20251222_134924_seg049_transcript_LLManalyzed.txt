While fear describes the inner attitude that must characterize an Israelite, the practical outcome of reverence to God is service. The service that is required of Israel is characterized by two Hebrew terms in sincerity and in truth. The first term is Tamim is mostly used as an adjective to describe the perfection of the sacrificial animal. Okay, that's sincerity. The second term that describes Israel's service is truth or faithfulness, the Hebrew emet. A faithful person is somebody who is dependable and trustworthy.
