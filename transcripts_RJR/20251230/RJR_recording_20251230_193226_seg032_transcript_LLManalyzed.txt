Grant distribution is expected to begin shortly following tens of thousands of damage assessments across affected parishes. Minister of Labour and Social Security Pernell Charles Jr. has announced that the Solidarity Program has begun disbursing its first tranche of grant payments. Under the Social Protection Program, beneficiaries are given a one-time cash grant of $20,000 and connected to long-term training, employment, and social services. Via a recent press release, Minister Charles outlined that the Solidarity Program demonstrates the government's commitment to ensuring that no Jamaican is left behind. He indicates that a second tranche of payments will be disbursed in January of 2026. Since the program's launch, solidarity fairs and community outreach activities have been held in St. Mary, Portland, Manchester, Kingston and St. Andrew, St. Elizabeth and Westmoreland, complemented by on-the-ground engagement from the ministry's social service teams.
