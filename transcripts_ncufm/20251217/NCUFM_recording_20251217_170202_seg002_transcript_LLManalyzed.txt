The Jamaica Public Service JPS says additional line workers from the Greystone Utility Services of Canada are expected to arrive in Jamaica tonight. In a release, the JPS informed that this is part of its continued efforts to accelerate electricity restoration for customers impacted by Hurricane Melissa. It says on Monday over 60 line workers from Greystone arrived in Jamaica and 40 line workers from Tempest Energy landed on Tuesday, joining their colleagues. JPS advised that the teams represent a significant boost in field resources focused on repairing and rebuilding damaged electricity infrastructure, now mainly in the hardest hit western parishes.
