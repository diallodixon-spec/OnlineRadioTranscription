In addition to medical monitoring, the program provides nutrition, education, and support packages that include hygiene supplies and mosquito nets. Mental well-being is also a key focus, with mothers being encouraged to speak openly about stress and seek psychosocial support. The ministry says the outreach is helping to strengthen care and build resilience for families as recovery continues. Health and wellness minister Dr. Christopher Tufton says it will cost approximately 7.1 million US dollars to restore operating theaters damaged by Hurricane Melissa at hospitals across Western Jamaica. Speaking during an online press briefing, Dr. Tufton said assessments were carried out at 13 operating theaters across five hospitals.
