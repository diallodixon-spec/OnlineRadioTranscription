Two British nationals who were nabbed in a suspected international cocaine ring were freed today of drug trafficking charges in the St. James Parish Court. Shelby Bing, an employment advisor, and Nanishha Nelson-Roberts, a student, were freed after their attorneys challenged the evidence presented against them. Prosecutors had reported that on August 16 last year, the two women were preparing to board a flight to the UK when they were stopped and searched. Fourteen pounds of cocaine with a street value of more than $30 million was seized in their luggage, concealed in coffee. But at the trial, which commenced in January, Ms. Bing's attorneys Christopher Townsend and Denise Gooden argued that she had no knowledge of the cocaine.
