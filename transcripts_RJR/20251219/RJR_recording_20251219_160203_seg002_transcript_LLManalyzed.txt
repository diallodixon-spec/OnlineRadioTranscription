Opposition spokesman on transport, Mikael Phillips, is seeking clarification from Transport Minister Daryl Vaz regarding the JUTC's move on to existing rural-stage carriage routes without observing the provisions of the laws governing rural transport or conducting any financial due diligence. In a statement today, Mr. Phillips said the clarification was necessary following the JOTC's announcement that it was abandoning its field express service route to Otorius and converting it to a regular Kingston to Otorius route. He pointed to transport authority regulations which require origin and destination surveys as well as seat assessments before a rural stage carriage license can be issued to an operator for rural routes.
