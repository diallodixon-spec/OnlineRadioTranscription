The Jamaica Constabulary Force says two teenage girls are being treated for bullet wounds believed to be the result of separate incidents of illegal gun salutes on New Year's Day. The JCF says detectives assigned to the St. Catharines South and Kingston Western divisions are working to determine the origin of the gunfire and identify those responsible. In one incident shortly after midnight, a 13-year-old Canadian national who was visiting relatives was standing on a balcony along Dreamland Drive in Portmose and Catherine observing fireworks celebrations when she was struck by bullets in her thigh and back. She was assisted to hospital where she has been admitted in stable condition. In the other incident, a 14-year-old girl from Bond Street in Kingston was injured after midnight while sitting beneath a tree in her community. The teen reportedly feeling a burning sensation to her shoulder, after which it was discovered she had been shot. She is in stable condition.
