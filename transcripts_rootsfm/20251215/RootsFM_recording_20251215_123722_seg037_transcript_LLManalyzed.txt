20251215 123722 Police have vowed to maintain public safety as the Christmas shopping season intensifies across the island, with increased foot traffic expected in town centres and commercial districts. The Jamaica Constabulary Force says additional patrols will be deployed to deter crime. Senior officers say the focus will be on theft prevention, traffic management and rapid response incidents. Police are also warning against careless cash handling and are urging businesses to strengthen security arrangements. The assurance comes amid concerns from citizens about pickpocketing and robberies during the busy holiday period. Lawmen say joint operations with private security companies and community watch groups will play a key role. Motorists are being reminded to obey traffic laws as congestion increases. Police have also encouraged the public to plan shopping trips carefully and avoid isolated areas at night.
