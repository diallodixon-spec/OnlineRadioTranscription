Their halos are made from discarded bicycle wheels, and the manger is an old tire. Two burn bins and a shopping cart flank the scene. Reverend Rachel Griffin-Allison says the arrangement reflects the lives of multiple people on the margins, like immigrants, refugees, and the unhoused. For her, the Christmas story is one of God choosing vulnerability over power. Putting this installation out is not adding politics. It's removing the filters that maybe made the story feel safe in the first place because it never was. Griffin Allison hopes the Nativity expands viewers' ideas of who is holy.
