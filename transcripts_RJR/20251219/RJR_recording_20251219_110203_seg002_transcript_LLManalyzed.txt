Councillor for the area, Audie Myers, has also apologized to the Health Department noting that he was shocked and disappointed by the actions of a small number of residents. Still in St. Elizabeth, residents of Santa Cruz and surrounding communities are calling for the National Water Commission to indicate when regular service will be restored. They have been without water since the passage of Hurricane Melissa. The residents pointed out that service has been restored to neighboring and adjoining communities. They say the prolonged disruption has become increasingly costly as they are forced to purchase water at exorbitant prices. The residents claim that the NWC has not provided trucked water during this period. Attempts to obtain a comment from the NWC on the matter have so far been unsuccessful.
