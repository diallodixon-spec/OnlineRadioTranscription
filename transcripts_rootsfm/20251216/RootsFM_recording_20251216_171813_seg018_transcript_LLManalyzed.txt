The Jamaica Council for Persons with Disabilities is urging that accessibility be prioritized in rebuilding efforts following Hurricane Melissa. Executive Director Dr. Christine Hendrick says the widespread damage caused by the storm presents an opportunity to rebuild using universal design principles, ensuring that buildings and public spaces can be safely used by everyone, including persons with disabilities. She stressed that accessibility should be built in from the start, pointing to standards in the National Building Code and a checklist developed by the JCPD to guide proper construction.
