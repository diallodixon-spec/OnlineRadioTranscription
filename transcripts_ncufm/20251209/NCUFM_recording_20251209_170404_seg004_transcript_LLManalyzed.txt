Minister of Industry, Investment and Commerce, Senator Aubin Hill, says his ministry has been working to get small businesses back on their feet after Hurricane Melissa devastated livelihoods and left many business operators without electricity and stock. On Thursday, the Small Business Association of Jamaica and the Private Sector Organization of Jamaica accused the government of dragging its feet in giving support to the business sector. Addressing the Senate on Friday, Senator Hill shared that his ministry has so far handed out generators to and restored retail stalls and shops across St. Elizabeth, Westmoreland, and St. James. He advised that over 1,200 businesses in Truloni, Hanover, St. Elizabeth Westmoreland and St. James. He advised that over 1200 businesses in Trelawny, Hanover, St. Elizabeth, Westmoreland and St. James were assessed to determine which businesses would need to be restored. Minister Hill explained that small businesses are essential to restoring normalcy in the supply and demand chain.
