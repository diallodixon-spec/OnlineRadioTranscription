In regional news, Haiti is taking fresh steps towards elections planned for 2026, with renewed efforts to strengthen women's equal representation in the democratic process. A new electoral calendar has been announced as transitional authorities and civil society groups emphasize the role of women in building credible and inclusive elections. Currently, women hold just over 22% of government positions, below the constitutional requirement of 30%. In response, women's groups supported by the Ministry of Women's Affairs and UN Women have pressed for reforms to close the gap. The Provisional Electoral Council has included measures in a draft electoral decree to reject candidate lists with fewer than 30 percent of women and to reward parties exceeding gender targets.
