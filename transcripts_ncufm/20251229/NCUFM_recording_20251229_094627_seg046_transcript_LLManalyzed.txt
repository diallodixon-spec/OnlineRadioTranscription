20251229 094627 John Wycliffe I want us to imagine England in the 1300s. The church was powerful, the Bible locked away in Latin and ordinary people, farmers, mothers, children, could not understand the words meant to give them life. Now into this world stepped a man named John Wycliffe, later called the Morning Star of the Reformation. Wycliffe was born around 1320 in Yorkshire, England. He grew up in a modest family, but his mind was sharp and hungry for truth. As he studied at Oxford, he saw something troubling. The church leaders spoke of power, wealth, and tradition, but the people were starving for God's word. Wycliffe asked himself, how can they grow in faith if they cannot hear God's voice
