The Ministry of Health and Wellness is assuring Jamaicans that chemicals being used in mosquito fogging exercises are safe and pose no risk to human health. Health Minister Dr. Christopher Tufton says fogging remains an effective and safe method to control mosquitoes, particularly following Hurricane Melissa, which has created conditions for increased breeding. He says the insecticides currently in use have improved over the years and are environmentally friendly. The minister is encouraging residents to allow fogging teams to carry out their work, noting that the activity helps reduce the spread of mosquito-borne illnesses such as dengue. He advises persons who may be sensitive to the mist to limit exposure by stepping indoors or covering their nose briefly until it passes.
