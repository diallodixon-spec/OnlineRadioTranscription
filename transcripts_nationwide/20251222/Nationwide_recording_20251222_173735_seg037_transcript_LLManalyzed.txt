S&P Global explains that the revision to stable reflects its view that the likelihood that Jamaica's credit worthiness could further strengthen within the next year has become more remote. It adds that Hurricane Melissa caused an estimated 8.8 billion US dollars in damage or around 40% of the country's 2024 GDP. S&P Global says while it expects the economy to recover as it rebuilds, based on the time it will take to do so, it expects the government's fiscal position to temporarily weaken as its spending needs increase. It also says Jamaica's debt burden will rise before continuing its long-term trajectory of decline.
