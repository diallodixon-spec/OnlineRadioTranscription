**Interview with Ellis Hammer-Bickford, Jamaica's National Men's Surfing Champion**

In an exclusive interview with Clint Williams, Ellis Hammer-Bickford, the newly crowned national men's surfing champion, shared his journey to the top of the sport. Hammer-Bickford, who started surfing at the age of five in Bull Bay, Jamaica, credited the Wilmot family for introducing him to the sport. "I was very fortunate to live in close proximity to them and be able to go there every day as a kid and learn to surf," he said.

Hammer-Bickford's passion for surfing drove him to pursue a professional career, and he has been working towards it for many years. He acknowledged the challenges of competing at the international level, where surfers have to adapt to different conditions and wave sizes. Despite these challenges, Hammer-Bickford has had a successful year, with a quarterfinal finish in Florida and consistent results in other events.

When asked about his experience at the World Surfing Games in El Salvador, Hammer-Bickford described it as "a really great experience" that allowed him to represent Jamaica and compete against the best surfers in the world. Although he didn't perform as well as he had hoped, he learned valuable lessons about equipment and strategy that will help him improve in the future.

Hammer-Bickford emphasized the importance of support from the Jamaican government and the surfing community to help him and other surfers achieve their goals. He expressed his desire to be on the podium and wave the Jamaican flag high, inspiring young people to take up the sport. With bigger plans for the upcoming year, including competing in events in Florida, California, and Hawaii, Hammer-Bickford is determined to qualify for the Olympics and put Jamaican surfing on the map.

**Interview with Dr. Jacqueline Becesa-Mackenzie, Chief Medical Officer, and Dr. Christopher Tofton, Health Minister**

In a separate interview, Dr. Jacqueline Becesa-Mackenzie, Chief Medical Officer, and Dr. Christopher Tofton, Health Minister, discussed the deployment of the Chinese hospital ship, the Silk Road Ark, to Jamaica to support hurricane recovery efforts. The vessel, which arrived in Jamaican waters on November 22 and departed on December 16, provided medical care to residents in the corporate area, Montego Bay, and Falmouth.

Dr. Tofton highlighted the significance of the Chinese government's support, stating that the vessel had a total of 104 medical professionals on board, including 60 doctors, 32 nurses, pharmacists, and technicians. The medical team completed outpatient procedures, accepted walk-in patients, and performed surgeries, dental, and medical procedures for patients and healthcare staff.

Dr. Becesa-Mackenzie commended the local healthcare staff for their support and coordination, which enabled the successful execution of the medical mission. She emphasized the importance of the vessel's presence in reducing the strain on local hospitals, particularly in hurricane-affected parishes.

The interview showcased the collaborative efforts between the Jamaican government and the Chinese government to provide critical medical care to those in need, demonstrating the power of international cooperation in responding to natural disasters.

**Interview with Jerson Lewis, Agricultural Science Student**

Jerson Lewis, a talented Agricultural Science student, shared his inspiring story of passion and dedication to farming. Despite his young age, Lewis has already developed a deep interest in understanding plants, livestock, and technology in agriculture. He credited his love for farming to his outdoor activities and his desire to contribute to the agricultural sector.

Lewis's achievements are impressive, having earned seventh place on the National Merit List for his performance in the 2025 Caribbean Secondary Education Certificate. He aspires to be a leader and make a positive impact in his community, and his love for farming has become a pathway to excellence.

The interview highlighted Lewis's determination to learn and grow, particularly in the face of challenges such as the destruction caused by Hurricane Melissa. His story serves as an inspiration to young people, demonstrating the importance of pursuing one's passion and making a difference in their community.
