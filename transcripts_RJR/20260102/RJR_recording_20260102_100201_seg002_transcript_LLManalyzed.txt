In a statement marking the new year, the GSF said it has been working in close coordination with the Haitian national police and military as efforts continue to bring peace, stability and security to the country. Several Caribbean countries have been rattled by earthquakes during the first two days of the year. The seismic research center says the latest tremor occurred early this morning with a magnitude of 3.5 and a depth of 10 kilometers. It was felt in Guadeloupe, Dominique and Martinique. On New Year's Day, a tremor with a magnitude of 3.7 rattled Trinidad and Tobago. Another earthquake yesterday with a magnitude 3.8 shook St. Kitts and Nevis, Antigua and Barbuda, also Montserrat.
