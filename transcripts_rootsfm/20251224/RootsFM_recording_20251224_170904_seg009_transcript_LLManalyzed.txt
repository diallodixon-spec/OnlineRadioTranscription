The United States Department of Homeland Security has announced plans to overhaul how H-1B work visas are granted, ending the long-standing lottery system. Under the new approach, visas will be prioritized for higher-skilled and higher-paid foreign workers. U.S. officials say the change is aimed at preventing abuse of the program and ensuring employers are not using the system to hire overseas workers at lower wages than Americans. The new selection process will use a weighted system that increases the chances for applicants tied to more sophisticated and better paying jobs.
