20251218 171208 Canada has recorded one of its largest population drops on record following a federal crackdown on international students. New figures from Statistics Canada show the population fell by 0.2% in the third quarter, dropping to about 41.6 million people. The only other quarterly decline occurred in 2020 during COVID-19 border closures. Officials say the latest fall is largely due to fewer international students after Ottawa moved to sharply reduce study permits. Non-permanent residents now make up 6.8% of the population, down from 7.3% last quarter. Prime Minister Mark Carney has pledged to cut that figure to 5% by 2027. The government plans to halve international student permits next year while modestly increasing permanent residency intake.
