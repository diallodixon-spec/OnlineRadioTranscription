Nigerian authorities say all school children and staff abducted from a Catholic boarding school in central Nigeria have now been released. Officials confirmed the rescue of the remaining 130 pupils and teachers taken from St. Mary's Catholic School in Papiri, Niger State, bringing an end to one of the country's largest mass kidnappings in recent years. More than 250 children and staff were abducted from the school on November 21st. Earlier this month, about 100 of the abductees were freed, with authorities working to secure the release of those still in captivity.
