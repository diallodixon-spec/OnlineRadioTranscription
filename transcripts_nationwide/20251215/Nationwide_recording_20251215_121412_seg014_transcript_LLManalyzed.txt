Energy Minister Daryl Vaz says the program will be implemented by Jamaica's Social Investment Fund. Some of the previously allocated $1 billion under the National Energy Poverty Reduction Project will be used to assist families severely impacted by Hurricane Melissa. This program, which will be implemented by JSEF between December 2025 and March 31st, 2026, focuses on restoring safe electrical access, repairing homes, providing house wiring for regularization, and supporting GER recertification and offering small solar solutions to vulnerable groups. Minister Vaz says the program is set to reach 3,000 households. It is expected to benefit thousands of households across seven parishes and 14 priority zones.
