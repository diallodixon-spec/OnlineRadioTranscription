Hoteliers are optimistic about the 2025-26 winter tourist season, which began on Monday after the tourist industry suffered a setback following disruptions caused by Hurricane Melissa. But President of the Jamaica Hotel and Tourist Association, Christopher Jarrett, says tourism is rebounding faster than expected. He says domestic tourism has always been a lifeline, pointing out how it helped keep many hotels going during the tough days of the COVID-19 pandemic.
