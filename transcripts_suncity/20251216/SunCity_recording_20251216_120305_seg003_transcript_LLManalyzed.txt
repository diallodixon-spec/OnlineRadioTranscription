The student loan bureau says more than 6,000 clients have already onboarded its newly launched debt reset program with about 3,600 applications submitted so far. Of those 800 borrowers applied for arrears credit, while nearly 2,900 sought the 100,000 good standing credit. Some 25,000 customers are expected to benefit overall. The limited time initiative offers full waivers on late fees and insurance charges, a 50% waiver on interest arrears for eligible accounts, a one-time credit of up to $100,000 for loans in good standing. The program is also open to Jamaicans living overseas who can register and manage payments entirely.
