In other news, the National Solid Waste Management Authority, NSWMA, says the massive cleanup operation following Hurricane Melissa remains one of the country's biggest recovery challenges. Nearly two months after the Category 5 storm struck on October 28, the NSWMA reports that between 4.8 and 5 million tons of debris are still being managed island-wide, the equivalent of almost half a million truckloads. In the five weeks immediately after the hurricane, crews cleared approximately 15,000 truckloads of debris, focusing mainly on fallen trees, damaged structures and organic waste, which officials say is critical to reducing health risks, and supporting rebuilding. To strengthen the effort, the government this week commissioned 10 new tipper trucks valued at almost $200 million, dedicated exclusively to hurricane cleanup operations.
