Hundreds of family and friends are now gathered at the National Indoor Sports Centre in Kingston for the official farewell celebrations of the life of late reggae legend, James Jimmy Cliff Chambers. Members of the music fraternity and dignitaries, including cabinet ministers and members of the opposition, are among those paying their respects to Jimmy, who brought global influence through his music. He died on November 24 from complications of pneumonia. He was 81. Prime Minister Dr. Andrew Holness, in his remembrance, described Jimmy Cliff as a cultural giant, a pioneer, and one of the most important builders of Jamaica's global identity. Jimmy Cliff was more than a singer, he was more than a songwriter, he was more an actor, he was more a performer.
