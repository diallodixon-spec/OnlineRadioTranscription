This bill is not about excusing wrongdoing. It is about creating an orderly, responsible, humane system that recognizes change, promotes rehabilitation and strengthens social reintegration. When people are allowed to rebuild their lives, Jamaica becomes safer, stronger, and more prosperous. When barriers to employment are removed, our economy grows. When individuals can legitimately pursue opportunities here or abroad, families are uplifted. And when we signal that rehabilitation is real real we reinforce the fundamental principles of justice and fairness. The bill first brought to the House on October 10 was passed with no amendments following a delay in debate contributions from members due to the passage of Hurricane Melissa.
