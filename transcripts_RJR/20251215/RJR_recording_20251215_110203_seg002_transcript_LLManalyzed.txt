Still in St Thomas, residents of Bath are urging the Municipal Corporation to investigate the construction of houses along the banks of the Planting Garden River. In a report to the Parish Development Committee, the residents highlighted that these dwellings are being built on gabion baskets originally installed to prevent the river from overflowing into the community. The residents expressed concern that the situation is worsening as some individuals are removing stones from the gabion baskets for personal use. They also noted that the heavy rainfall from Hurricane Melissa has already left at least two houses precariously close to the riverbank. Vendors at the Lewisville Market in St Elizabeth are calling for the Municipal Corporation to indicate when work will begin to renovate or properly cover the market facility, which was extensively damaged during the passage of Hurricane Melissa.
