Swinging over to cricket as Indian teenager Vaibhav Sheravanshi has rewritten the record books after becoming the youngest player to score a century in Men's List A cricket. Aged just 14 years and 272 days, Sheravanshi reached three figures off only 36 balls. In Bihar's Vijay Hazard Trophy opener against Arunachal Pradesh, he went on to smash 190 from 84 deliveries, breaking AB de Villiers' long-standing record for the fastest 150 in list A cricket by reaching the milestone in just 59 balls. His knock included 16 4s and 15 6s and helped Bihar post a staggering 574 for 6. The highest total in men's list A history and only the second time a team has crossed over 500 in a 50 over innings.
