The Jamaica Business Development Corporation, JBDC, which operates Things Jamaican, says buying local remains one of the most effective ways to stimulate economic recovery. Acting Chief Executive Officer Harold Davis notes that every dollar spent on Jamaican-made products helps to keep businesses afloat, secures the income of families, generates tax revenue for the government, and conserves foreign exchange through the multiplier effect. Golden Cross Caribbean Bakery and Grill says Hurricane Melissa has disrupted plans to significantly increase its imports of agricultural produce from Jamaica this year. The company says it was on track to import about $500,000 worth of Jamaican produce, roughly $80 million in local currency, up from about $470,000 last year before the storm's impact on the agricultural sector.
