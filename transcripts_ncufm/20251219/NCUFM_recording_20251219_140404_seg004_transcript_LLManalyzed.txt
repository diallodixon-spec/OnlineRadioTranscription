And finally in Athletics, the organizers of the controversial Enhanced Games says the inaugural event will challenge long-held views on doping in sport, despite widespread criticism from medical experts and anti-doping bodies. The enhanced games will allow athletes to use substances banned in mainstream competition under medical supervision as they compete for large financial prizes. The first event, featuring sprinting, swimming, and weightlifting, is scheduled for May 2026 in Las Vegas. Organizers argue that regular medical monitoring and long-term health screening will reduce risks, but that view has been strongly disputed by experts, who warn of serious long-term health consequences. Olympic medalist Ben Proud and world champion sprinter Fred Curley are among the athletes who have signed up so far.
