The artifacts, 62 in total, include a traditional Inuit kayak, along with tools and household items such as needle casings, a soup ladle, and an Ulu knife. They were formally handed over by Pope Leo XIV to the Canadian Conference of Catholic Bishops, following years of calls by First Nations Inuit and Matisse leaders for their repatriation. Indigenous leaders welcomed the artifacts on their arrival in Canada, describing the return as an important step in addressing the Catholic Church's historical role in the suppression of Indigenous culture. The items had been part of the Vatican's ethnographic collection for about a century, after being sent to Rome by Catholic missionaries in the 1920s.
