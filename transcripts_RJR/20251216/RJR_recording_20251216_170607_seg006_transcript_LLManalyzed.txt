And the Small Business Association of Jamaica, SBAJ, is calling for the OUR to rescind its approval of the 7 percent electricity rate increase. SBAJ President Garnett Reid, speaking with Radio Jamaica News today, described the increase as unconscionable and questioned the timing, particularly during the Christmas season. Mr. Reid warned that the hike could force many businesses to close and lead to higher prices for consumers, noting that electricity is a critical input in the production of goods and services. He also raised questions about the US $150 million being lent by the government to the JPS for power restoration and highlighted the US $5 million provided by the OUR to JPS from the Electricity Disaster Fund.
