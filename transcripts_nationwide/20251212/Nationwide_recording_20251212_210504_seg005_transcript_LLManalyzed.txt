Dr. Tufton was speaking at a press conference held at the ministries office in New Kingston on Thursday. The National Health Fund is reporting that 90% of public pharmacists have resumed services. Health and Wellness Minister Dr. Christopher Dufton notes that the remaining 10 percent of the public pharmacies which suffered major structural damage are currently delivering services through a mobile unit. Since November 1, mobile units have filled approximately 4,440 prescriptions for over 3,500 patients in 62 remote communities across western Jamaica. Since the December 11th and Friday December 12th, the mobile units will be operating in St. James, Cambridge, Hanover, Chester Castle and Mount Pito, Westmoreland, Cornwall Mountain and Big Woods.
