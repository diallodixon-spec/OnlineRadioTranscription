Meanwhile, the Bank of Jamaica BOJ says conditions are in place to support a gradual recovery in the coming years despite the challenges brought by Hurricane Melissa. BOJ Governor Richard Bowles says that although economic activity contracted sharply in the immediate aftermath of the storm, reconstruction efforts and inflows of external financing are expected to help drive renewed growth. The central bank notes that recovery activity will be supported by funding from multilateral institutions, private sector investment and disaster risk financing mechanisms.
